4. Material Contrariety (Determinate Negation) Specific Rejection

The Logic: Red is not Blue.

Anti-Racism requires content. It is not enough to say a policy is ‘bad’ (vague). We must posit a specific alternative that excludes the inequity.

Example: Rejecting a biased reading list by specifically adding voices that correct the historical record.

5. Formal Contradictoriness (Abstract Negation) The Empty “Nơ’

(Defined by what it isn’t)

(Defined by what they are)

The Logic: Red is not Not-Red.

Educational Context: Colorblindness. Saying “I don’t see color” is an abstract negation. It wipes away the student’s reality without offering a material alternative.

It is a logical trick to avoid the work of equity.

6. Metadifference: Determinate vs. Abstract Active vs. Passive Stance

Abstract (Passive)

“I treat everyone the same.” Ignores friction. No content.

Determinate (Active)

“I am redesigning my grading rubric to remove bias.” Engages friction. Has content.

Equity requires Material Contrariety (Action), not just Formal Contradictoriness (Words).

7. Universals and Particulars The Label vs. The Student

The Deficit Ideology Trap: Seeing a collection of Universals (stats) instead of a Particular (human).

Brandom: Universals are repeatable properties. Particulars are unique occurrences.

The teacher’s error is often treating the Particular only as a collection of Universals.

8. Particular as ‘Also’ (Medium) Intersectionality

The student is a ‘Medium’ where many identities coexist. These traits are not mutually exclusive; they live together in the person.

9. Particular as “Exclusive One” (Unit) Agency and Integrity

The student is not just a passive bucket of traits. They are an active Unit that repels incompatible properties. When a teacher projects a false label, the student resists. This is “Pushback”—a sign of the student’s integrity.

10. Difference in Opposites

Systems Clash; People Just Are

The Abstract

The Reality

Universals have Opposites.

The Particular has no Opposite.

Don’t treat the student as a battleground for opposing theories. They are a unique reality to be engaged with.

The Struggle for Recognition From Master to Ally

Mastery / Savior Complex

Reciprocity / Solidarity

We must move beyond acting on the community (Mastery) to acting with the community (Recognition). Service learning requires reciprocity.

Confession, Forgiveness, and Trust

The Spirit of Trust in Education

Equity Literacy isn’t about being perfect; it’s about repair. We build a community of trust by acknowledging our errors (Confession) and reconstructing the relationship (Forgiveness).

“The forgiveness which it extends to the other is the renunciation of itself… the word of reconciliation is the objectively existent Spirit.”
